Hi, my name is Aaron.

I'm a data scientist and ecologist.
I currently work in Durham, NC on projects related to forests and water.



About Me

My resume:

I am a statistician interested in forests and water, data science, and entrepreneurship. As a researcher at Duke University and Colorado State University, I have initiated, collaborated on, and completed scientific projects to measure international greenhouse gas emissions and predict forest responses to climate. My academic work is published in multiple peer-reviewed journals.

I have a Ph.D. degree from Duke University, a M.S. degree from Colorado State University, and a B.S. degree from Beloit College. I am interested in applying my analytical skills to help people better manage their relationships with their data and the environment.

I also love restoring my old house in Durham, making maps, climbing, and doing yoga.

Projects

My current scientific projects are focused on the impacts of trees on the water cycle and the consequences of changing forests for water supplies and quality. I like to combine data from environmental sensor networks with mathematical models. Recently, I examined the response of forests to drought with colleagues, where we predicted tree death after droughts. I have also worked on global greenouse gas budgets to understand how agricultural practices affect nitrogen cycling.

I am continually thinking about how my work can add value for people. I was involved in a project to develop an advanced system for measuring forests with remote sensing and unmanned aerial vehicles that is easy to use for both small and large forest landowners. This project provided knowledge that could someday be used for rapid forest inventories and reliable carbon accounting.

Teaching

I am excited about helping students learn science by doing what scientists do—collecting and analyzing data, synthesizing information, and communicating results to a variety of audiences.

I have developed multiple inquiry-based educational modules for undergraduate and graduate students and I encourage active exploration and discussion in my lectures and exercises. I have taught courses at Duke University and Beloit College, including courses on plant biology, global change biology, and environmental science. I have also given guest lectures in data science and environmental statistics courses at Duke.

Also at Duke, I have co-organized a working group on environmental data analysis and visualization with Matt Ross. Lessons for that course are on my Github page.
