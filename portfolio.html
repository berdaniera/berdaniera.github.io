Case study 1: La Vita Yoga

La Vita Yoga is a streaming video web application for a yoga studio in Durham, North Carolina. It was built to respond to the COVID-19 pandemic and help the studio move online.

As the product lead, I developed the application to meet two primary user needs:

  1. remotely access a subscription video library and,
  2. register and pay for live yoga classes.
I gathered design input from the studio owner and talked to yoga students to understand their points of friction. For the initial prototype we developed a manual process, including:
  • handling payment through Stripe,
  • granting on-demand video access through Vimeo, and
  • manually emailing Zoom class links to students.
This prototype and interviews with over 40 users led to the development of a web application that automated each of these steps and made browsing videos and registering for classes easier. Additional experiments that were ultimately removed include: a referral program, Spotify audio along with videos, and a “plus” subscription with expert guides.

Early user feedback:

  • “Beautiful layout.”
  • “Love the videos and think this is such a great resource!“

Case study 2: TransLoc OnDemand

TransLoc OnDemand is an administrative application that powers the “microtransit” portion of the TransLoc mobile application. In this system transit riders book trips through the TransLoc mobile application, which TransLoc processes and dispatches to public transit agency vehicle drivers.

As a product manager, my focus was on improving the rider experience for the existing applications. I worked with public transit agency buyers to understand their trip scheduling needs (balancing wait times with the number of vehicles in service). Then, I clustered rider/user feedback from the mobile application (thousands of users) to prioritize their concerns.

As part of a cross-functional product team of software engineers and product designers, we used these inputs to iteratively develop multiple consumer-facing capabilities, including:

  • Indicators in the mobile application for the expected drop-off window (pre-request, shown at right) and expected wait time (pre-confirmation) to help riders make a more informed trip decision.
  • Routing optimization to make vehicle routing more efficient (reducing vehicle miles/cost) and reduce rider wait and ride times.
  • Dispatcher prioritization capabilities to reconcile customer support of trips that need urgent attention.
  • Administrator “hub” points to support trips that connect to or from other modes of transit and highlight those locations for riders.
